Euphoria is a peak of intense joy — the split second after a goal, the roar of an entire stand, the embrace shared with a stranger. In football this emotion is never solitary: it is collective by nature, binding thousands of people into a single emotional body that transcends age, background, and language.

The Starting Point

Not Nostalgia.
Identity.

Collective Euphoria was born from a personal childhood memory and a collective conviction: supporting Juventus is not a demographic trait — it is a language passed down across generations. An invisible code connecting fathers and daughters, cities and stadiums, kits and memories. This project brings that code back to the surface — in market stalls, on city walls, on every screen.

One Goal.
One Roar.
One Identity.

The Four Initiatives

One Vision.
Four Forms.

Editorial Collection

C.O.D.E.

Core of Discipline & Elegance. A collectible editorial album inspired by the Panini tradition, dedicated to Juventus history, iconic players, and club imagery. Each pack is a pocket archive — illustrations, stats, and defining moments from 1897 to today.

Heritage Streetwear

Class of 1897

A limited-edition heritage jersey in recycled polyester piqué (rPET), released annually in a strictly numbered run. Only 1,897 pieces — one for each year since the club's founding — a wearable document of Juventus identity.

Progressive Web App

Bianconeri

A retro arcade video game built on Juventus club history, accessible as a PWA across all devices. A digital platform that transforms club culture into a playable, shareable experience — updated every season.

Urban Activation

Urban Visual Manifestos

Wheatpaste poster installations across Turin, Milan, and Palermo. Artist-led works that speak the visual language of football fandom — turning city walls into open-air galleries for the Bianconeri community.

The Community

Community

Collective Euphoria speaks to everyone who carries Juventus as part of their identity — not just on matchday, but in everyday life, in the objects they keep, the things they wear, the stories they pass on.
